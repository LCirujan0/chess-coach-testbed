Roadmap

Five user journeys ordered by what closes the daily training loop first. Each loop has steps that already happened, are happening, or come next. The full per-feature spec list still lives in docs/super-app-roadmap.md + engineering/handoffs.md; this page is the human one.

Foundation in place
  • Core puzzle loop: ingest Chess.com games → Stockfish-grounded mistakes → no-spoiler coach with reveal-mode after 3 fails (v0.1 + v0.10–v0.12)
  • Coach calibrated to your live Chess.com rapid rating (v0.10 — Engineering Spec 01)
  • Daylight visual system, 5-surface nav, mobile + desktop layouts (v0.2)
  • Insights surface with rating trajectory, component tiers, per-theme breakdown, mistake heatmap (v0.3 — Engineering Spec 04)
Loop 01 Close the daily training loop Now
Today the loop is ingest → solve → next puzzle. To make it sticky we need the loop to diagnose what you're weak at, target the puzzles, and let you see the lift. The Insights surface is in but its signal is approximate until two small captures + a motif classifier land.

Loop 02 Coach as conductor Next
One coach reads your games, names what's leaking, prescribes a daily session, and re-aims as you improve. Deterministic ranking in code (zero tokens, auditable) + one small LLM call to narrate. Ships in two slices: the per-game story for Game Review, and the daily "Coach's pick" on the Practice hub.
  • ·
    Game Review surface — tap an ingested game, walk through it move-by-move with the coach narrating mistakes; "Drill this motif" CTA into Puzzles.~2 days · Spec 05 game-narrative slice · depends on Loop 01 motif tags
  • ·
    Daily "Coach's pick" on the Practice hub — one ranked next-step from the deterministic diagnosis, narrated by the coach.Spec 05 daily slice · design in design-explorations/practice-system.html
  • ·
    Re-aim as you improve — the ranking is pure recompute, so lifting one component automatically reorders the priorities.No extra build; falls out of the conductor logic
Loop 03 Deepen the four pillars Queued
Tactics already get real signal from your own games. The other three pillars — calculation, endgames, openings — need a curated source because your own games either avoid the topic (openings you don't play) or surface clean examples too rarely (endgames). Each pillar plugs into the one Themed Practice surface, not a side mode.
  • ·
    Endgame trainer — 30 curated positions (basic mates / pawn / rook / minor / mixed). Play vs Stockfish, mastery = 2 clean wins in a row.~2 days · curriculum drafted in docs/chess-knowledge.md · dataset job next
  • ·
    Visualisation training (Mode B) — see → hide → list moves → question → replay. Six levels.~1–2 days · the calculation pillar your own games can't reach
  • ·
    Vienna opening repertoire — top ~100 lines from Lichess Masters DB, drill mode + per-node SRS.~2 days · external data acquisition is the long pole · stays research until item 5 ships
Loop 04 Supply — promote when your own games run thin On demand
Your own-game puzzles are personalised but finite. When the queue starts repeating, you'll want supply that's rated and unlimited. The dataset is already prepared and waiting on the bench.
  • ·
    10,500-puzzle Lichess pack (validated, balanced 14 motifs × 750, bundled, offline). Adds a "Mine | Lichess" source toggle, line-based grading, zero tokens per Lichess puzzle.~1 day from cold · dataset at engineering/data/lichess-puzzles.json · Spec 03
Loop 05 First impression — the 60-second aha Designed
If the app ever leaves the testbed, the first run matters more than any feature. Stream the coach's narration as it finds your mistakes; surface the cleanest, most teachable position first (not just the first one found); contrast reveal + "your three biggest leaks" → Start today's session.
  • ·
    Streaming onboarding from Chess.com username → first aha position in ~60s, with a coach typing indicator instead of a spinner.Design ready in design-explorations/onboarding-aha.html · build when scope shifts toward public
Loop 06 Make it portable Parked
Stays parked while it's a single-user app. The hinge: if we point this at anyone else, both items move up immediately because shared localStorage stops being acceptable.
  • ·
    Auth (Supabase) — email + password, JWT, per-user data in Postgres with RLS, localStorage → Postgres migration on first sign-in.~3–5 days · required before sharing the URL
  • ·
    Persistent coach memory tied to user_id — top-3 next-things every 50 puzzles, surfaced on the Today screen.~1 day once auth is in
