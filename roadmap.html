Where KnightPath is headed

Roadmap

KnightPath turns your own games into a daily training habit: it finds your real mistakes, sets them as puzzles, and coaches you through them without giving the answer away. Here is what works today, what we are building, and what is coming.

Working today
  • Bring in your Chess.com games and turn your real mistakes into puzzles to solve
  • A coach that guides you toward the move without spoiling the answer
  • One consistent training screen for every kind of exercise
  • Progress insights: your strengths and weak spots, an estimated rating, and a practice heatmap
  • A daily session to work through
Live now Train on your own games, with a coach by your side Available
Everything you need to practise is already here. KnightPath learns from the games you actually play and turns them into focused practice.

    Bring in your Chess.com games and turn your real mistakes into puzzles, so you practise the positions you actually got wrong.
  • ✓

  • ✓
    One consistent training screen for every exercise type — mistake puzzles, playing an endgame out against the computer, and judging whether a position is winning, drawn or losing.
  • ✓
    Progress insights that show where you are strong and where you leak points by phase of the game, plus an estimated rating and a heatmap of your practice.
  • ✓
    A daily session, so there is always something to work through.
Building now A daily session that flows from one exercise to the next In progress
Right now your daily practice can feel like separate stops. We are stitching it into a single, smooth session that carries you from one exercise to the next.
  • →
    One session that moves you through every kind of exercise in the same place, so you never have to jump between screens.
  • →
    A progress bar that stays with you, with your results tracked as you go.
Coming next Practice that targets your weak spots, guided by your coach Next
Once the daily session flows, we want it to be smarter about what it gives you — and the coach to play a bigger role in steering your practice.
  • ·
    A daily plan that aims at your specific weak spots first, while still keeping the rest of your game sharp.
  • ·
    The coach reviews a game and tells you how you played, with a one-tap "drill this" to practise any mistake it points out.
  • ·
    Your "winning, drawn or losing" results feed into your insights, so they shape what you practise next.
